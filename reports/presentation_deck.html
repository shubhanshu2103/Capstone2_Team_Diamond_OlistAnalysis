📈 Review Score Distribution

Key Observations

• Heavily right-skewed — majority are 5-star reviews

• Score of 1 is the second most common — polarized distribution

• Customers tend to rate at extremes (love it or hate it)

📈 Delivery Delay Distribution

Key Observations

• Most orders arrive early (mean delay ≈ −12 days)

• Only ~6.8% of orders arrive late

• Sellers set conservative estimated dates as a buffer

📈 Delay vs. Review Score

Key Observations

• Clear inverse relationship — higher delay → lower score

• 5-star reviews: avg delay = −14 days (early)

• 1-star reviews: avg delay = +3 days (late)

📊 Hypothesis Test: Late vs On-Time

Result: REJECT H₀

• On-Time orders: Mean score = 4.29 (n = 89,949)

• Late orders: Mean score = 2.27 (n = 6,410)

• Difference of 2.02 points is highly significant

• Late delivery nearly halves the review score

🔑 Key Findings

1

Delivery drives satisfaction

Late deliveries receive scores ~2 points lower than on-time (2.27 vs 4.29)

2

Statistically significant

Pearson r = −0.267, p < 0.001 — delivery delay negatively correlates with review scores

3

93.2% on-time rate

Most orders arrive early, but the 6.8% that are late drive disproportionate negative reviews

4

Multi-factor satisfaction

Delivery explains ~7% of variance — product quality, communication also play key roles

5

Geographic disparities

Some states face significantly higher late delivery rates — logistics optimization needed

💡 Recommendations

🚚 Logistics Optimization

Target high-delay states with improved carrier partnerships and regional fulfillment centers to reduce late deliveries.

📦 Proactive Communication

Implement real-time delivery tracking and proactive delay notifications to manage customer expectations.

📊 Predictive Monitoring

Build a delay prediction model using order & logistics features to flag at-risk orders before shipping.

⭐ Recovery Programs

Launch targeted recovery campaigns (discount codes, priority support) for customers who experienced late deliveries.
