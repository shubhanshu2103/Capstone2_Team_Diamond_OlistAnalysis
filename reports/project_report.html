1. Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive analysis of the Olist Brazilian E-Commerce dataset to investigate the relationship between delivery performance and customer satisfaction. Using 96,470 delivered orders from 2016–2018, we applied a rigorous data analytics pipeline including extraction, cleaning, exploratory analysis, and statistical testing.

Key Result: Delivery performance is a statistically significant predictor of customer satisfaction. Late deliveries receive an average review score of 2.27 compared to 4.29 for on-time deliveries — a difference of 2.02 points on a 5-point scale (p < 0.001).

The Pearson correlation coefficient between delivery delay and review score is r = −0.267, confirming a moderate negative relationship. A linear regression model shows that each additional day of delay reduces the predicted review score by 0.034 points, although delivery delay alone explains only 7.1% of the variance in review scores, suggesting other factors (product quality, communication) also contribute to satisfaction.

2. Introduction & Problem Statement

2.1 Background

E-commerce has grown rapidly in Brazil, with the market projected to exceed $40 billion by 2025. Olist, a major Brazilian marketplace connecting small merchants to large retail channels, processes orders across all 26 states and the Federal District. Customer satisfaction is critical for marketplace retention and growth.

2.2 Problem Statement

"How does delivery performance (on-time vs. late deliveries) impact customer satisfaction (review scores) in the Brazilian e-commerce ecosystem?"

2.3 Research Questions

  1. What is the distribution of customer review scores and delivery delays?
  2. Is there a statistically significant correlation between delivery delay and review score?
  3. Do late deliveries receive significantly lower review scores than on-time deliveries?
  4. Can delivery delay predict review scores using linear regression?
  5. Which geographic regions experience the most delivery delays?

2.4 Objectives

  • Build a reproducible ETL pipeline to clean and merge 6 source datasets
  • Conduct exploratory data analysis with 7+ visualizations
  • Perform statistical hypothesis testing (Pearson correlation, independent t-test)
  • Fit a predictive linear regression model
  • Generate actionable recommendations for Olist's logistics operations

The Pearson correlation coefficient of −0.267 indicates a moderate negative relationship between delivery delay and review score. As delivery delays increase, customer satisfaction decreases. The p-value of effectively zero confirms this relationship is not due to random chance.

The linear regression model review_score = 3.75 − 0.034 × delay_days shows that delivery delay is a statistically significant predictor, but the low R² (7.12%) indicates that delivery delay alone does not fully explain customer satisfaction. Other factors — such as product quality, seller responsiveness, and product-expectation mismatch — contribute to the remaining 93% of variance.

7. Key Findings

  1. Delivery performance is a critical driver of customer satisfaction. Late deliveries receive review scores approximately 2 points lower than on-time deliveries (2.27 vs. 4.29).
  2. The relationship is statistically significant. Pearson r = −0.267, t = −132, both with p < 0.001.
  3. 93.2% of orders arrive on time or early, with an average of 12 days ahead of the estimated delivery date.
  4. Only 6.8% of orders are late, but these drive a disproportionate share of negative reviews and 1-star ratings.
  5. Order volume showed strong growth from 2016 to 2018, indicating a healthy and expanding marketplace.
  6. Geographic disparities exist — some Brazilian states experience significantly higher late delivery rates than others.
  7. Delivery delay alone explains ~7% of review score variance, confirming that while delivery is important, satisfaction is multi-factorial.

8. Recommendations

8.1 Short-term

  • Proactive Communication: Implement real-time delivery tracking and proactive delay notifications to manage customer expectations when delays occur.
  • Recovery Programs: Launch targeted customer recovery campaigns (discount codes, priority support) for orders identified as delayed.

8.2 Medium-term

  • Logistics Optimization: Partner with additional carriers in high-delay states. Consider regional fulfillment centers to reduce transit times.
  • Seller Training: Provide sellers with data on their delivery performance and best practices for faster order processing.

8.3 Long-term

  • Predictive Delay Model: Build a machine learning model to predict delivery delays at order time, enabling proactive interventions.
  • Multi-factor Satisfaction Model: Expand analysis to include product returns, seller response times, and product category to build a comprehensive satisfaction prediction model.

9. Limitations & Future Work

9.1 Limitations

  • Temporal scope: Data covers 2016–2018 only; market dynamics may have changed since then.
  • Single predictor: The regression model uses only delivery delay; a multi-variate model would be more predictive.
  • Review bias: Customers with extreme experiences (very happy or very unhappy) are more likely to leave reviews, which may skew the distribution.
  • Missing review text analysis: Natural Language Processing (NLP) on review comments could reveal additional satisfaction drivers.
  • No return/refund data: The dataset does not include post-delivery return or refund information.

9.2 Future Work

  • Incorporate NLP sentiment analysis on review comments
  • Build a multi-variate regression or gradient-boosted model for satisfaction prediction
  • Add seller performance metrics as additional features
  • Conduct geographic clustering to identify logistics improvement zones

10. Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates a clear, statistically significant relationship between delivery performance and customer satisfaction in the Olist Brazilian e-commerce marketplace. While the majority of orders arrive on time, the small percentage of late deliveries has a disproportionately negative impact on customer reviews. The findings provide actionable insights for logistics optimization, customer communication improvements, and predictive monitoring systems that could meaningfully improve the Olist customer experience.

The complete analysis pipeline — from raw data extraction through statistical testing — is fully reproducible using the provided notebooks and scripts, ensuring transparency and auditability of all results.
